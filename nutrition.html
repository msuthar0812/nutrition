Nutrition

Balanced Diet: Emphasize the importance of a balanced diet that includes a variety of nutrient-rich foods, such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Encourage portion control to maintain a healthy weight.

Fruits and Vegetables: Highlight the benefits of consuming a wide range of colorful fruits and vegetables, as they are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. Encourage individuals to aim for at least 5 servings per day.

Whole Grains: Recommend incorporating whole grains like brown rice, quinoa, whole wheat bread, and oats into meals. Whole grains are a good source of fiber and can support heart health.

Lean Proteins: Encourage the inclusion of lean protein sources such as poultry, fish, legumes, and tofu. They provide essential amino acids and support muscle growth and repair.

Healthy Fats: Educate about the importance of choosing healthy fats like avocados, nuts, seeds, and olive oil. These fats can help support heart health when consumed in moderation.

Limit Sodium and Added Sugars: Advise reducing the intake of sodium and foods high in added sugars, as excessive consumption can increase the risk of heart disease and other health issues.

Hydration: Promote the importance of staying hydrated by drinking an adequate amount of water throughout the day. Encourage individuals to limit sugary drinks and opt for water as the primary beverage.
